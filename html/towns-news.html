  • The priestess' cry

    The illustrations of Alba Aureum are an example of the beauty of vector graphics. “The prietess’ cry” is considered to be the “Mona Lisa of the Illustration” by the Illuste Day – for the position of her eyes, while she is crying. She captures the suffering with such “blue-sky and orange-skin” beauty.

  • The ancient dragons.

    Dragon (n.) early 13c., from Old French dragon, from Latin draconem (nominative draco) "huge serpent, dragon," from Greek drakon (genitive drakontos) "serpent, giant seafish," apparently from drak-, strong aorist stem of derkesthai "to see clearly," from PIE *derk- "to see." Perhaps the literal sense is "the one with the (deadly) glance." (Source:Etymonline)

  • A wonderful place to visit.

    Are you wondering where to have a nice day? Then, you should look at Externstein, in Germany. Besides the pure air of the forest and the harmonious atmosphere that you will have for walking, the rocks of the Externstein are mysterious pieces of archeology. The rocks themselves have an interesting shape, what makes them more attractive. It is said, that it was built to be a place to study the stars, quite like an observatory nowadays.

  • Natura morta is trendy again.

    Were you wondering which style to choose? Why not to try with a couple of fruits and digital aquarelle? You would be surprised how easy and beautiful it can look like. A nice app, that enables you to do it, is Adobe Sketch. Adobe Sketch has a user-friendly interface, so you can begin directly with the drawing and send it after, if you want, to Adobe Photoshop and make changes. What are waiting for?
